This doc captures search strategies and source categories for finding more themes in this vein.

Intellectual Tradition — Primary

Augustinian / Western Christian Contemplative

The core tradition Contueri draws from. Spans the 4th century through the Reformation. Themes are interior, universal, and art-generative.

Neoplatonism

The philosophical root. Plotinus's Enneads is the primary source — themes of The One, Beauty, Emanation, Return. Plato's dialogues are the ground beneath that.

Classics of Western Spirituality (Paulist Press series)

The single most on-target reference source. This book series covers Augustine, Meister Eckhart, Julian of Norwich, John of the Cross, Hildegard of Bingen, Bonaventure — every major figure whose themes would feel native to the app. Each volume's table of contents is a ready-made theme list.

Candidate Themes to Explore

Drawn from the tradition above — not yet in Sanity, worth researching further.

  • Beauty — Platonic/Augustinian kalon; "our heart is moved by beauty toward the beautiful"
  • Longing — desiderium; the ache toward the transcendent; John of the Cross
  • Darkness — apophatic theology; "dark night of the soul"; unknowing as a path
  • Time — Augustine's meditation on time in Confessions Book XI; Boethius
  • Stillness — hesychia (Greek); Eckhart's Abgeschiedenheit (detachment/stillness)
  • Water — purification, baptism, living water (John 4); universal spiritual symbol
  • Ascent — Platonic ascent toward the Good; Bonaventure's journey of the mind to God
  • Creation — Francis of Assisi; the world as theophany; Hildegard's viriditas
  • Grief — compunctio (holy sorrow); tears as spiritual gift in Desert Fathers
  • Fire — Pentecost; purgation; "our heart was burning within us" (Luke 24)
  • Thresholds — liminal moments; death, dawn, crossing over
  • Memory — Augustine's Confessions Book X; memory as the seat of the self
  • Breath — pneuma/spiritus; the animating principle; prayer as breathing
  • Wonder — thaumazein (Aristotle); the origin of philosophy and prayer
  • Solitude — Desert Fathers; the cell as school of the soul
