Palette Structure — Two Paths

Same six fresco pigments, two ways to organize them. The structural choice matters more than the colors themselves.

Option A

Single polychromatic palette

All six jewel tones available on every screen. Heroes are always artwork; jewels live in the frame around the art — gallery hairline, rule below title, badge, primary CTA, active tab. Reading surfaces stay parchment + ink. The product has one consistent visual identity that pulls from the full fresco.

Roles on one screen

  • Saffron — gallery hairline (frame around art)
  • Mauve — gold rule below title
  • Verdigris — badge
  • Vermilion — primary CTA
  • Sistine blue — active tab
  • Parchment — reading surface

Trade-off

Cohesive identity across the whole product — the artwork stays the focus, jewels frame it. Risk: still busy if all six show on every screen. Strict discipline required: each color has one job, never two.

Option B

Theme-driven accent groups

Each Journey inherits its own 2–3 fresco pigments as its visual identity. Reading surface stays parchment + ink across the whole app; the jewels change by content. Content variety becomes a visual feature.

Trade-off: No single “KALLOS color” — the brand becomes the fresco grammar (parchment surface + jewel-tone moment per Journey) rather than one specific hue. Risk: requires editorial discipline to assign pigments per Journey at content time. Upside: the app feels like a museum where each room has its own light.

The pigments in both options. Mauve #8E6E7B, Saffron #D69A2D, Sistine blue #34468C, Powder blue #7B9BC4, Vermilion #C8543C, Verdigris #5F7A6B, Raw umber #7B5536, Parchment #EFE2C4. Hexes drawn from the colors you circled in the School of Athens.

Reading-surface rule (both options). Page background is always parchment. Body text is always ink. Jewels live only in: hero blocks, gold rules, badges, primary CTAs, and active state indicators. Saturated color on a long reading surface kills readability — and breaks the “contemplative” register.

The differentiation from AI-meditation apps. Headspace, Calm, Aro, Opal, Insight Timer all use earth-tone surface + one accent (gold or sage). Both options here use parchment + multiple saturated jewels. That polychromatic grammar is what reads as art rather than wellness.
