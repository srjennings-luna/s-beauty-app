Mockup roles (both surfaces, identical): saffron gallery hairline frames the artwork; mauve thin rule under the title; verdigris badge; vermilion CTA on espresso, umber CTA on parchment; Marian powder blue as the active tab dot.

What works on espresso: all six pigments hold up. Saffron, vermilion, and Marian read brightest; mauve, umber, verdigris read deeper. Cream text on bright pigments (saffron) needs espresso ink, not white.

What needs care on parchment: saffron and Marian powder blue are too light for CTAs against cream text — for buttons on parchment, drop to a deeper variant (saffron → #B4801F, Marian → #5C7FA8). Used as rules, badges, and active dots they're fine at full strength.

Why Marian beats Sistine here: Sistine ultramarine #34468C reads as institution on parchment (bank/airline). Marian powder blue #7B9BC4 reads as fresco sky — softer, more pictorial, more contemplative. Better fit for the register KALLOS wants.
