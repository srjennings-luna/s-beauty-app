They Were Early

In the 2nd century, most people assumed the new Christian faith and Greco-Roman philosophy were enemies. Justin Martyr wrote directly to Emperor Antoninus Pius and argued the opposite: the Greek philosophers had not been wrong. They were early.

What is the most surprising thing you have ever discovered was true?

In Rome around 150 AD, you could be tried and executed for being a Christian. The rumors about what happened at their secret gatherings had been spreading for years.

What's the same in both. Body text (cream-dim Open Sans), prompt question (cream Cormorant italic), Lectio quotes (cream Cormorant italic), the audio play-circle (cream with espresso glyph), the bottom-nav active state (sage), the hero image, and all type sizes/weights. Layout is identical.

What changes — five spots, one rule each. Section labels (DAILY PROMPT, READING, MUSIC, ACTION, VERBA, GO DEEPER, attributions, dates, Music pill, READ MORE, LISTEN) shift sacred gold → aged gilt. Hairline rules and box borders shift sacred gold → verdigris. The Curator-Note left bar shifts to verdigris. The BEGIN button shifts from flat gold to the verdigris→gilt gradient. The page background shifts from flat espresso to the Whisper gradient.

Active tab stays sage. Functional UI state, not brand decoration.

Parchment screens (Explore, Journeys list, Library) still flat. Whisper is the espresso-mode treatment. Parchment is the reading-surface mode; flatness is its job. Separate decision if you want a parallel light treatment.
