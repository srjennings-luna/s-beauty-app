What each says. Plaster reads as fresco wall — warmest, most architectural; pairs visually with the espresso Whisper’s mauve top because both are dusky earth-tones. Verdigris breath reads as patinaed stone — coolest; carries the verdigris brand pigment through to parchment surfaces so the splash → daily-app verdigris thread becomes consistent. Manuscript reads as aged paper — warmest and brightest; closest to the “old book” or “archive” register.

My read. Verdigris breath is the strongest move for system coherence — it makes verdigris the through-line on both surfaces (rules + CTA gradient on espresso splash, atmospheric tint on parchment screens). Without it, parchment screens feel disconnected from the splash. But it’s the most subtle, and could read as “just slightly off-cream” rather than “intentional patina”.

If you want more presence, dial up. Each gradient can hold a stronger start color (e.g. #E8E3CC for plaster, #E8E8D5 for verdigris, #F4E5BC for manuscript) without breaking readability. Tell me which direction reads right and I’ll tune the saturation.
